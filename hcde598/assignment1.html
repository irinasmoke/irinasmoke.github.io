Laser Cut Cardboard Construction Kit

For this assignment, we had to make a cardboard construction kit using a laser cutter. I made a 28 piece construction kit containing two shapes - circles and Ls.

Brainstorming & Quick Prototyping

I started this assignment by looking up examples of construction kits. I sketched out different shapes I was considering for the kit and made quick cardboard cutouts to get a sense of how the shapes might fit together.

Iterations

Eventually, I landed on two shapes - circles and Ls. The circles had 3 notches and the Ls had 2. Next, I created the digital versions and did a first print

Once I was happy with the design of the pieces, I made digital versions of them and cut some test pieces along with a piece with various sized notches to test the fit. The first cut I made used 50% power and 50% speed settings, and did not cut through the cardboard. I adjusted the settings to the ones suggested in class - 100% power and 25% speed - and that worked well.

Next, I tested the fit of the pieces. I tested various slot sizes - .10, .15, .175, and .20 ths of an inch - and found that .15 slot size worked best for the kind of cardboard that I had, which according to my calipers was .16 inches thick.

Even with the adjusted slot size, I still found that the L shapes were bending when I fit them together. To fix this, I mitered the corners to make them slide together more easily and made the shape thicker overall so that it would be harder to bend. These changes worked well

Final Result

Once I was satisfied with the shapes and their fit, I cut out 14 of each shape, for a total of 28 pieces, and put them together in various ways. If I had more time, I would have printed more pieces so that I could make larger structures, and different shape variants so that I could try out more types of structures.
